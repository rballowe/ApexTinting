Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between carbon and ceramic tint?
Carbon window tint contains carbon particles that help to absorb and disperse heat. Carbon is a great option to help reduce the amount of light and heat that enters the vehicle, but only offers moderate UV protection. In addition, carbon tint more affordable than ceramic.

Ceramic tint has non-metal, nano-ceramic particles, which is more effective at blocking heat and UV rays. Cermaic blocks a higher percentage of UV rays, allowing for even more cooling of one's vehicle. Although ceramic is more expensive, it does provide excellent clarity and non-reflective appearance.


How long does it take for my tint to be attached?
It depends on customer needs and preference. For example a small to mid sized car could take anywhere from 2 to 2.5 hours. Larger vehicles can take anywhere from 2.5 to 3.5 hours. Time is also dependednt upon whether the customer wants the entire car tinted, or just a single window. In addition, services can take longer if there is previous tint that needs to be removed first. Please contact us to discuss your specific needs.


What is the AZ law for window tint?
The law states that you can have tint on your windows but under specific guidelines. AZ law permits non-reflective tint ont he top 5 inches of the windshield. Tint on the front and side windows must allow at least 33% of visible light to pass through. Rear and side windows have no restrictions.

AZ law also allows for medical exceptions, which would be offer darker tint. You can review the law here, along with details for the medical exception.


What is the difference between the percentages?
The term "perentage" in the context of window tinting describes the amount of light that can pass through the tint. A lower percentage indicates a darker tint, while a higher percentage results in a higher tint. For instance, 5% represents the darkest possible tint, while 70% is the lightest.

It's important to note that as the tint percentage increases, the effectiveness of UV protection decreases.


Is there anything special I need to do for my tint after it has been applied?
Let it Cure: The tint needs time to cure and it can take 2-4 days. During this time, avoid rolling down the window as it can cause damage

Cleaning: Use a gentle cleaner and a non-abrasive sponge.

Damages: If you notice any scratches or damages to the tint, contact us to make necessary repairs.

By following these care tips, you can help ensure that your window tint remains in good condition and continues to provide the UV protection and enhanced privacy benefits you desire.
